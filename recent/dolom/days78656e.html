Days 7-8

By Igor Talevski
Friday, 8 September, 2006

Cinque Torri, Punta Fiames...

6 June
The weather was still not getting better and it was again cloudy. We decided to wait again on Punta Fiames route and that day we went on Cinque Torri. We chose two routes and they were located on Torre Barancio which top rises up to 2308m height. The first route that we did was on the North Face and it is called “Dibona”. It is graded IV and it has 4 pitches. The second one is the North-West Corner and it was also graded IV with 3 pitches (see Rating Systems). They were both first climbed by the Dibona/Apollonio/Stefani party in September 1934. The difficulties on the first route were located in the fist half where we climbed through well-polished slabs; the intermittent rain was not helping either. But after that the terrain eased up and I ended the last pitch on the Torre Romana from where we made one 50m abseil. I liked the second route much more. It had a sustained difficulty level and again (like the day before) I moved through a well-sized corner/chimney.

- Cinque Torri

The chimney is actually in the crack between the two towers, Torre Barancio and Torre Romana - they are connected at the foot but the higher you go they split very visibly as seen from the second route.. When we finished climbing the first route it started raining and I thought that that would be it for that day. But actually the rain eased up and we decided to do a quick climb of the corner no matter how wet we would get. The moment when we started climbing it stopped raining, and the moment I collected the rope after the abseil it started raining again. It was a special moment, if felt like we were somehow rewarded for our decision…

7 June
The next morning the skies looked clear. They were much clear and bright and we decided to go on Punta Fiames. The mountain is 2240m high, and the route is on its south face called “ Via Comune”. It is a 400m long route with 15 pitches graded IV+ (see Rating Systems). After the approach (which we had researched a few days earlier) we came under the wall and started looking for the real chimney, at the foot of which it was written that the route starts. I think we started out about 3-4 meters to the left but anyway we came to the first belay stance and from there on, the route finding was easy. I was well absorbed in the climbing of the route and at one moment I felt I knew where the first party would have gone the next pitch, my guess turned out to be correct… It was nice climbing and we liked this route very much also. I must admit that I had doubts whether we should start or not since when we arrived the sky was covered with gray clouds and it looked like it would rain again. But we had delayed enough, and it was the second time we had made the approach – I guess we would have never come for a third time so we just started and later the sky cleared up a little. We both like climbing long routes no matter how easy/difficult they are. We like being exposed on a wall for few hours and being concentrated on the climb and on the difficulties that we are passing through. It is a special moment when you come down in the camp and look at the big face again and remembering how you went through the corner, and then the exposed traverse, then chimneys… I don’t know why – it is simply so. From the top of the route there was a short via ferrata (over fixed steel cord) to the summit from where first over a cairn marked path and then just by orientation we came to the gullies that were scree-filled and after 2 hours we were at our car.


         

- Punta Fiames

So we did nine routes in eight days during this first visit of ours in the Dolomites. It was a nice experience and we hope to visit this places again very soon since there are so many possibilities in a very vast and real alpine climbing area. We liked it especially because most of the time we were alone and climbing by ourselves in a nice (and sometimes wild) mountain environment was a special experience.


With utmost gratitude for his inspiration we dedicate these climbs to Sri Chinmoy.

Previuos

Thinking or Dreaming of Swimming The English Channel?

By
8 September, 2006

Don't Dream - JUMP!!

To anyone who reads this, who may have any tiny drop of interest in swimming the English Channel...

When I first met Sri Chinmoy, I was quite astounded by the stories I read of his students who had swum The English Channel. You must understand that I come from a very competitive swimming and triathlon sports background. And make that SPRINT distance events! Well, yes, I did eventually get into the Ironman Triathlon after many years of sprint racing. But relative to The Channel, even that swim is a sprint! The Channel was far beyond my imagination, so I thought!

In my journey to swim The English Channel, these SCMT swimmers were often my inspiration.

This is the reason.

Many of them had little or no competitive swim training background at all. So I desperately made them talk to me about just how they managed to swim across The English Channel! Hopefully, we will have all their stories on our website -soon! They helped me much more than they know.

If you enjoy swimming. If you like to train. If you like to eat! If you have some swimming background. If you find yourself constantly drawn to the challenge of swimming across this beautiful and powerful body of water, then I challenge you to try to begin your training and plans.

My very good friend, Vasanti from Germany, trained for (alot) less than 1 year before her crossing. Most know of her amazing determination and faith. A group of boys from New York, Adhiratha, Anugata, Dhruva, Sunil and many more took on the challenge with far less swimming background than most recreational lap swimmers. They found not only success, but some very fast times! We watch Karteek every year face difficult swims with a new regiment of training and focus. And then there is Vedika who has swum 22 and 23 hours to complete her crossing!

When Sri Chinmoy asked me to swim The English Channel, I was so surprised. I thought he was joking with me. But no, there were specific instructions and I tried to follow them. Looking back, I still can not believe it was me climbing the rocks on the shore of France. It was an experience beyond anything I had in all my life. And it changed my life. It is so hard to explain. Better you should experience it yourself!

My EC training team was Sri Chinmoy, Vasudha, and myself. Bigalita was my cheerleader. Dhavala, Shayamala, and Bhagirathi were my added as my Channel Crew. There were lots and lots of obstacles along the way. The training was hard and yes, I did get fat! But we all had a victory that would not have been possible without one of the team.

So You Dear Swimmer!

Build a team. Make a plan and have some fun. The journey to The English Channel crossing is just about the best part!

Several people have sent messages that I should swim The Channel again. I feel it is time to look for new challenges.  But I am comitted to helping any of you that need help. I will post all the information that I can to our Channel Swimmers website. 

Next year is the 20th Anniversary of our team's first English Channel crossings by Vasanti and Adhiratha.

(Have I mentioned that already?)

Somehow I have the thought that we should send 20 SCMT swimmers to cross The Channel next year. If that team included a relay of girls and a relay of boys, only 8 more would be required to attempt their solo swims. Ok... we could add anther relay and have 2 solos, but I know many more will be inspired to solo swim!

 So, what do you say swimmers?
Don't worry... I was surprised and challenged when I heard it first too!!

San Francisco is an EXCELLENT place to train for The Channel. The water is very similar in temperature and it is easy access to Aquatic Park where crazy open water swimmers swim all year long. AND Ananda Fuara can't be beat for gaining that extra "Channel Fat"! Venu's neatloaf is magical Channel Swimmer fuel...

And we need an Australian or two! Such a swimming powerhouse country! They should be represented.

My roommate, Jayashri, has a deposit in and is willing to build a team of girls for a relay. Igor (Ukraine), I think you are committing to a swim. Vasanti is considering an "anniversary swim crossing". Who else?
Karteek will be going for his 7th crossing and Vedika too.

Looking forward to your questions and building a team for 2005!

_
Ahelee
Peaceful Waters
San Francisco

Channel News Tuesday August 10

By
8 September, 2006

Update #2 on Vijaya's crossing.

Another report on Vijaya who is swimming The Channel at this very moment!

The conditions are excellent. A record in the men's crossing was nearly set today by Spanish swimmer, David Meca demonstrating to good water. 7 hours, 35 minutes. They say he is not a big swimmer but fast. He had trouble with the cold. Imagine that!

Vijaya is swimming with Pilot Mike Oram who has assisted with her previous attempts and knows the Sri Chinmoy Marathon Team swimmer's philosophy well.

He says her swim is going quite smoothly. In fact at the 1/4 and 1/2 way points she is better along than ever. Mike says, if she doesn't talk on the feedings every 30 minutes, she is well on to a sub-20 hour swim! But swimmers know, FAST feeds are really key to a successful crossing. A very good day for her so far.

Keep swimming Vijaya, our brave warrior sister! This time, the water is your battlefield!
