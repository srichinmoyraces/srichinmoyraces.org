Back from California

By Anonymous
Friday, 8 September, 2006

I just returned home from a few days of travel... only to find a WHOLE SLEW OF NEW ENGLISH CHANNEL HERO CHAMPIONS!!!

Congratulations to each of you!

I broke out of the fog and traveled to Southern California. The beautiful sunny weather and a stunning ocean swim in Santa Barbara that I got in, really brought back the memories of all the great Channel training from a few years ago. But alas, here I am, back in the fog and gray of San Francisco. This however, is the place where "real" Channel Swim Training happens every day of the year.

A note came the other day from South End Rowing Club president, Bill Wygant, that their Alcatraz Invitational this Fall is nearly sold out. Lynne Cox will be the master of ceremonies. And we hope to have all the swimmers over to our Ananda Fuara Restaurant for food and gab that weekend too! Would love to see any of you near by there! Saturday, October 2nd. Come for fun, even of you don't swim. September/October is by far the most beautiful time of the year in San Francisco. 

Ali and friends, thank you for the updates on everyone! I don't think I will ever get over HOW SENSATIONAL it is too see the progress reports of the crossing swimmers!

Does someone know where these girls doing the 3-way are from?

Hope the weather holds out for all of you coming up to swim now! We'll be watching from the around the world for updates!
Good Luck!

Ahelee Sue Osborn

Acclimation 2005

By
8 September, 2006

Even if the plans are not yet set, this is the best time to begin to acclimate your body for open water swimming next Summer!

There are lists and lists of what to do. Books to read. Websites to surf. People to chat with about experience. Ice-cream to eat. New swimsuits, goggles, and swim caps to buy.

But there is only one thing that will ultimately help you be ready for the cold water of your open water swim in 2005 and that is,

SWIMMING IN COOL COLD WATER!

 

The very easiest way to acclimate to cold water swimming is to start now. Before the water goes frigid cold in your nearest open body of water. Stay in the water through the Fall season and the Winter if possible. Even if it is for short swims or dips. You will find that your reaction to the cold water will not be as drastic as when you miss 3 months and the drop of 10-15 degrees and suddenly dive back in next year!

Now I'm not telling the Eastern Europeans to cut a hole in the ice to swim... but try to find ways to get yourself in cold water. Cold showers, outdoor pools, feet dipped in icy water - it all works wonders!

Lets hear some of your creative cold water acclimation stories!!

20 Summers Ago...

By
8 September, 2006

Sri Chinmoy Marathon Team Swimmers began journeys to Dover to swim The English Channel.

The first member of our SCMT to swim across The English Channel was Vasanti Neimz from Germany. Today she reminded me that it was 20 years ago that our first swimmer made his attempt to cross The Channel. As the story goes, many of Sri Chinmoy's students were there at the beach to see him off. The crossing however, was not to be that day.

The next year, Vasanti went on to her Channel victory and the next day, Adhiratha Keefe also reached the shores of France! There was a big celebration and a great tradition began.

I'm sure Vasanti never imagined she would see a day like today. Cheering on one of her teammates to the 34th English Channel crossing for The Sri Chinmoy Marathon Team!

20 Year Anniversary for Vasanti and Adhiratha... how about beginning to train for a special anniversary tribute swim - across The English Channel!
