1983 Sri Chinmoy Grand Prix

In Summer 1983 a second Grand Prix was held as part of Sri Chinmoy's birthday celebrations. From July 9th through to September 9th, Sri Chinmoy completed more than 50 time trials. On several days, he completed several physically exacting time trials, sometimes in close succession. Many of them were part of the Grand Prix series, while a few were solo races. The sprints were held at Jamaica High School track, at St. John's University track and even on the road in front of his own house. Sri Chinmoy also began to use spikes and starting blocks for some of the most significant sprints.

Sri Chinmoy's Sprinting - A Short Background

In 1970 Sri Chinmoy introduced annual Sports Days for his students to encourage them to maintain a high level of physical fitness. Sri Chinmoy has inspired his students to train seriously for the many track and field events that are regular items. Each year he has traditionally participated with his students and many of his best marks in the West have been centred on this day and the training leading up to it.

Mac Masters Meet 1983




On June 5, 1983 Sri Chinmoy ran the 100 metres in 14.84 at the Mac Masters Meet. The meet was held at St Johns University in New York.

The race was electronically timed. Sri Chinmoy was 61 years old and weighed 152 pounds. He finished second.
