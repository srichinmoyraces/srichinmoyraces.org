Sri Chinmoy Masters Games 1990



Sri Chinmoy ran 15.47 in the 100 metres at the Sri Chinmoy Masters Games on July 14, 1990.  Sri Chinmoy defeated long time friend and student Trishakash - pictured on the left. Sri Chinmoy weighed 155  pounds and took 72 1/2 strides.

1981 SRI CHINMOY GRAND PRIX


A special Grand Prix series of 31 races on 31 consecutive days was held during Sri Chinmoy's birthday celebrations in August 1981 to celebrate his 50th birthday. Beginning on July 27th, the races ranged in distance from 100 metres to 5 miles and the cumulative mileage for the Grand Prix came to 50 miles.

Sri Chinmoy competed in every one of the 31 races, recording many personal best times in the West. The shorter sprints were held on the cinder track at Jamaica High School, while longer events were held on roads in the area, such as 150th Street, or at nearby Flushing Meadow Park. 1983 Sri Chinmoy Grand Prix

In Summer 1983 a second Grand Prix was held as part of Sri Chinmoy's birthday celebrations. From July 9th through to September 9th, Sri Chinmoy completed more than 50 time trials. On several days, he completed several physically exacting time trials, sometimes in close succession. Many of them were part of the Grand Prix series, while a few were solo races. The sprints were held at Jamaica High School track, at St. John's University track and even on the road in front of his own house. Sri Chinmoy also began to use spikes and starting blocks for some of the most significant sprints.
