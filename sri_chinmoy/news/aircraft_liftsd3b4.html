76 Year-Old Sri Chinmoy Lifts 7 Aircraft

page created by Jwalanta Voelckner — last modified 2007-08-02 09:42 AM

July 29, 2007 [Shirley, NY] – Amidst roaring aircraft engines, rumbling thunder and astonished ground crew, 76 year-old fitness champion Sri Chinmoy power-lifted 7 light aircraft this afternoon in a massive demonstration of strength and inner will. Sri Chinmoy lifted a total of 16,189 pounds in just a couple of hours—including a Schweitzer glider aircraft with just his right arm at 545 pounds while seated!

Sri Chinmoy's seated one arm lift of the Schweitzer glider.

Dozens of enthusiasts and fitness advocates joined the strongman at Brookhaven Calabro Airport in Shirley, New York to witness this feat of sheer determination and concentration.

Age-defying athlete Sri Chinmoy said, “I am 76 years old. I live in the heart. When we live in the heart, we have no obstructions, no barriers. If we live in the heart, where there is oneness, nothing is impossible. Everything is possible."

Using custom-made apparatuses, Sri Chinmoy lifted a Schweitzer I-26E glider at 545 lbs. with his right arm from a seated overhead position; then 4 light aircrafts [CT Light Sport at 1014lbs, Evektor Sportsar 750 at 980lbs, 1942 Culver Cadet at 1149lbs, RV4 at 1214lbs] overhead on a modified standing calf-raise machine, and two air show planes—a Globe Swift at 2252lbs and Xtra 300 at 2455lbs--on a standing calf-raise platform.

Today’s lifts have capped an extraordinary journey for Sri Chinmoy. Now having lifted a total of 33 airplanes, he has paid tribute to the role of this class of airplanes in aviation history. Not only the pioneers to modern jet aviation, light aircraft continue to play a vital role in search and rescue missions.

Jim Smith, registrar of the British Amateur Weightlifters Association and noted strength expert, said “Sri Chinmoy is a marvellous example of what human beings have the potential to do.”

Five-time Mr. Universe and renowned fitness expert Bill Pearl said, “Sri Chinmoy is still achieving at age 76 what the greatest strength athletes could never do in their prime!”

In July 2003, Sri Chinmoy celebrated the “Century of Flight” at Boeing Field in Seattle, Washington by lifting overhead 100 pilots and presenting each of them a special award – the Lifting Up the World with a Oneness-Heart medallion. He, too, was honored on that day as an Honorary Member of the Civil Air Patrol of the United States Air Force.

THE V WORLD MASTERS GAMES

By Atulya Berube
26 July, 2012

IN SAN JUAN
PUERTO RICO
SEPTEMBER 23RD TO 30TH, 1983

At the request of the organising committee, Sri Chinmoy competed on the Puerto Rican team. He had been made an Honorary Citizen of Puerto Rico in 1976. Sri Chinmoy was invited to open the Games with a silent meditation and he also composed a special anthem for the occasion.

At age 52, Sri Chinmoy competed in the 50-54 year age category.


100 metres   
Friday, September 23rd   
Sixto Escabar Stadium
Sri Chinmoy's official finishing time:  14.48 seconds

Notes: The heats began at 4:40 pm. Sri Chinmoy's race number was 506. He chose not to wear spikes but he used Newton starting blocks purchased in Puerto Rico. His bodyweight was 133  pounds. The number of strides was approx. 64. This was Sri Chinmoy's fastest time in the West until 1992.

200 metres   
Sunday, September 25th   
Sixto Escabar Stadium
Sri Chinmoy's official finishing time: 31.40 seconds

Notes: The heats began at 4:30 pm. Sri Chinmoy was on the inside lane. He used his starting blocks. His bodyweight was 135 pounds.

400 metres   
Tuesday, September 27th   
Bithorn Stadium
Sri Chinmoy's official finishing time: 1:12.66

Notes: The heats were scheduled for 3:00 pm. Sri Chinmoy was in the first heat, which was the fastest. He qualified for the semi-finals, but he was not informed and did not run the following day. His bodyweight was 135 pounds.


For many years, these three times stood as the highlight of Sri Chinmoy's sprinting achievements in the West.
